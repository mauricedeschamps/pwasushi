Sushi Types

Nigiri

Pressed sushi with toppings on rice

Gunkan

Battleship sushi wrapped with nori

Maki

Rolled sushi with various fillings

Side Dishes

Common side dishes in sushi restaurants

Sushi Essentials

Essential items for enjoying sushi

Nigiri Sushi

Maguro (Tuna)

Lean red meat of tuna with mild flavor. A sushi classic.

Chutoro (Medium-fat Tuna)

Medium-fatty tuna with perfect balance of flavor and texture.

Otoro (Fatty Tuna)

The fattiest part of tuna with melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Salmon

Orange-colored with rich fat content. Mild and popular.

Ebi (Shrimp)

Sweet flavor with soft texture. Often served cooked.

Ika (Squid)

Chewy texture with subtle sweetness.

Hotate (Scallop)

Sweet and buttery with delicate texture.

Anago (Saltwater Eel)

Soft texture with sweet soy-based glaze.

Saba (Mackerel)

Rich flavor, usually served with vinegar cure.

Tamago (Egg)

Sweet layered omelet, a traditional finish to sushi meals.

Gunkan Sushi

Uni (Sea Urchin)

Rich, creamy texture with sweet ocean flavor. A delicacy.

Ikura (Salmon Roe)

Bursting texture with salty-sweet flavor. Usually soy-marinated.

Negitoro (Tuna Scallion)

Minced fatty tuna with green onions. Rich and flavorful.

Kani Miso (Crab Innards)

Rich and creamy crab innards with intense umami flavor.

Tobiko (Flying Fish Roe)

Small, crunchy eggs with mild salty taste. Often colored.

Mentaiko (Spicy Cod Roe)

Spicy marinated cod roe with distinctive red color.

Maki Sushi

Tekka Maki (Tuna Roll)

Simple roll with tuna. Named after gambling parlors.

California Roll

Inside-out roll with crab, avocado and cucumber. Western-style.

Kappa Maki (Cucumber Roll)

Refreshing cucumber roll. Named after a water spirit.

Negitoro Maki

Minced fatty tuna and green onion roll.

Ebi Tempura Maki

Crispy shrimp tempura inside sushi roll.

Sake Maki (Salmon Roll)

Simple roll with fresh salmon.

Natto Maki

Fermented soybean roll with sticky texture.

Kanpyo Maki

Dried gourd strips simmered in sweet sauce.

Ume Maki

Pickled plum paste roll with shiso leaf.

Side Dishes

Chawanmushi

Savory egg custard with dashi broth. Silky texture.

Miso Soup

Traditional soup with tofu and seaweed. Classic accompaniment.

Tempura

Lightly battered and fried seafood/vegetables. Crispy texture.

Edamame

Young soybeans boiled in salt water. Popular appetizer.

Sunomono

Vinegared salad with cucumber and seafood.

Sashimi

Sliced raw fish without rice. Highlights pure fish flavor.

Sushi Essentials

Gari (Pickled Ginger)

Thinly sliced young ginger pickled in sweet vinegar. Served as a palate cleanser between different types of sushi.

Wasabi

Japanese horseradish with a sharp, pungent flavor. Used as a condiment for sushi. Real wasabi is rare and expensive - most is a mix of horseradish and food coloring.

Matcha (Green Tea)

Powdered green tea often served at sushi restaurants. Helps cleanse the palate and aids digestion. Has a slightly bitter, earthy flavor.

Soy Sauce

Essential dipping sauce for sushi. Use sparingly to avoid overpowering delicate flavors.

Shari (Sushi Rice)

Specially prepared vinegared rice that forms the base of all sushi.

Nori (Seaweed)

Edible seaweed used to wrap sushi rolls and gunkan.

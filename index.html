Sushi Guide

Sushi Types

Nigiri

Pressed sushi with toppings on rice

Gunkan

Battleship sushi wrapped with nori

Maki

Rolled sushi with various fillings

Side Dishes

Common side dishes in sushi restaurants

Sushi Essentials

Essential items for enjoying sushi

Nigiri Sushi

Maguro (Tuna)

Lean red meat of tuna with mild flavor. A sushi classic.

Salmon

Orange-colored with rich fat content. Mild and popular.

Ebi (Shrimp)

Sweet flavor with soft texture. Often served cooked.

Gunkan Sushi

Uni (Sea Urchin)

Rich, creamy texture with sweet ocean flavor. A delicacy.

Ikura (Salmon Roe)

Bursting texture with salty-sweet flavor. Usually soy-marinated.

Negitoro (Tuna Scallion)

Minced fatty tuna with green onions. Rich and flavorful.

Maki Sushi

Tekka Maki (Tuna Roll)

Simple roll with tuna. Named after gambling parlors.

California Roll

Inside-out roll with crab, avocado and cucumber. Western-style.

Kappa Maki (Cucumber Roll)

Refreshing cucumber roll. Named after a water spirit.

Side Dishes

Chawanmushi

Savory egg custard with dashi broth. Silky texture.

Miso Soup

Traditional soup with tofu and seaweed. Classic accompaniment.

Tempura

Lightly battered and fried seafood/vegetables. Crispy texture.

Sushi Essentials

Gari (Pickled Ginger)

Thinly sliced young ginger pickled in sweet vinegar. Served as a palate cleanser between different types of sushi.

Wasabi

Japanese horseradish with a sharp, pungent flavor. Used as a condiment for sushi. Real wasabi is rare and expensive - most is a mix of horseradish and food coloring.

Matcha (Green Tea)

Powdered green tea often served at sushi restaurants. Helps cleanse the palate and aids digestion. Has a slightly bitter, earthy flavor.
